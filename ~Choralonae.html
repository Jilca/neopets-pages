A prayer

One day I'll get out of this place A soft voice was nearly overpowered by the rain, and the young woman sat limp against the wooden frame of her encasing. The canopy over the display overflowed with water, and she pulled herself away from the bars to avoid getting splashed. Many of the towns people walked free outside, slumped over in the rain as they hurried to and from, she wished so badly she could be out there, free, too. They can't keep us in here forever.

Oh, yes they can. a voice spoke that had been seasoned, weathered like the sign out front that had been there for decades. She sat inwards, as far away from the outer facing side of their enclosure, as if she had given up on the outside completely.

Nari, let the girl dream. another woman chimed in, she was laying down, nearly sleeping.

I'm not dreaming. The first girl responded, leaning back against the posts. She outstretched her hands to try and reach the rain. I've been praying to Cho, she added, One of these days we're all going to get out of here, I mean it... I can feel it.

That dragon has better things to do than concern itself with us. Nari sighed.

That's not true, the girl snapped back, She was one of us once.

The weathered woman's lips tightened, and she slouched deeper into the corner of the enclosure, letting the rain drown away her voice. That's just a fairytale.

The dragon in the casino

When the rainy season came, it suffocated the city. A flurry of water blurred the vision of anyone unlucky enough to be caught outside, droplets dancing violently in the whipping winds. In the center of the city, a Casino stood as a beacon of warmth, its dazzling lights seen brightly even through the crowded air. But as warm as it may look, only the gamblers dared enter this place, even if it could offer refuge from the storm.

For at the peak of the tower, there was a suite with gold, jewels, clothing and other dazzling items strewn across the floor carelessly, and a beast that called this home. The yakuza typically did not let anyone higher than the 7th floor, let alone the peak of the tower. Few had access to this room, and even fewer left it alive. A dragon lived in its riches here, and it had little patience for disruptions.

The entrance to the top floors was guarded by two massive gold doors. Every day, the gamblers that traveled from lands across the world to visit the dragon's casino, touched the door, prayed, and chanted, hoping the dragon may bless their money with good luck.

Its name was Cho, and although the gamblers were the only ones brave enough to enter this place, there was a time when the citizens of this city once prayed to this mighty dragon, for love, for freedom, for protection.

But prayers can only go unanswered so long, before even the desperate will give up.

Nobody knows where the dragon came from. There are rumors of course, there always is. It came from the mountains, it came from the ocean, it came from hell. Wherever it came from, this city would be a whole lot brighter if it just went back was something often said through whispers on the dirt roads. That thing only cares about money, it doesn't give a damn about us.
