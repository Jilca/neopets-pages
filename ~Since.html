In the low candlelight of what appears to be an unkempt but rather quaint old attic two silhouettes can be seen. One is of a slim young woman perched on several stacked boxes that resemble a throne. The other is man of unidentifiable age hunched in the corner, peering at the woman behind an old grandfather clock as if not wanting to be seen but waiting to be noticed.

The woman is first to speak.

Come closer. Don't be afraid. We all have something we desire, don't we? I suppose you heard about me through a friend, though I cannot vouch for everything he has said. I can only assume you know my intentions as well as I know yours.
Youth? Beauty? Health? What is it that you want most?
Or do you want nothing but to see with your own two eyes the legitimacy of the rumors?
I can assure you if it is the latter we have nothing more to discuss, not even for a sum of money that would let me settle into a comfortable retirement.

W-why not?! I have plenty of money to offer you! Name your price!

Ah, I see. You are after that after all. You see, my services are not to provide knowledge. Knowledge is so limiting; knowing the truth, but not the whole truth, can distort your mind so great that you are no longer faced with ignorance but a greater enemy: the illusion of knowledge.

I refuse! I have paid people great money to tell me what they know of your location! I flew to five different cities! Goodness me, I even had to pay someone to tell me you live in the attic of some old clothing store! I will not leave without what I want.

Sir, you will not have it. Please leave.

Tell me your secrets or I will strangle the livin—gackhhuugnnn!!

You seem to believe persistence can conquer all. Care to give death a try?

The man doubles over, some strange cross between a choke and a scream escaping his lips as he brings his hands up to his neck. Veins flash in the light, contrasting harshly against the soft glow of the candles. Some unseen force appears to be smothering all the oxygen from the man's lungs but aside from the woman who is several feet away no one else was in sight.

No one else...until you look down and see your own fingers, transparent and ghastly, squeezing his throat with all your might.

Since: A Character Study

Name: Cecilia Diane Amielle
Alias: the Fortune Forger *tentative title
Age: Twenty three years
Zodiac: Pisces; compassionate, intuitive, gentle, reactive

History: Cecilia was raised in a large family of eight children in a small town at the border of a city that taunted its neighbors with flashing lights and busy roads. She adapted her love for fabrics from her aunt, who tailored expensive wedding dresses. Every holiday her family was invited to her villa and as the adults partied she found herself in the studio, studying the frills and laces on each gown. Though her aunt hadn't specifically forbade her from entering her private work area, she always felt the inkling of trespassing whenever she snuck away from the dinner table.

One night when she was caught her aunt had discovered her lying in the midst of her best dresses, entangled in string and cloth and beads. She wasn't sure what drove her away exactly, perhaps it was the shame, perhaps it was the panic...perhaps it was the strange revelation of entering someone else's soul that she could not shake, that planted a seed of fear in the back of her mind.

Over ten years later Cecilia returns to her town to find that the shop had been abandoned due to bankruptcy and most other nearby shops deserted as well. She took temporary refuge in the attic but made a home out of the small living space when she found herself unable to detach from the only thing she has left of her family. Cecilia has had the strangest attachment to dresses, even though they bring her the solemnest despair, ever since.


Those eyes. Those eyes only turned red when Cecilia was trying to see into another person's past and drawing out the bits he wished revived. The dramatization of the effort required made her appear almost saintly; the red of her pupils made her seem almost as if in pain. Her hands would burn as if she were using them to sift through the oldest cinders in Hell as she tore and plucked at the person's past—hence the gloves.

She often described going through a person's past like diving. The invisible force that blew her back like a wind often left her hair in shambles after the affair. She has since kept it short and tied with a bow that often did little to confine the strands.

Cecilia wore the heaviest gown she could find at all times. The thick cotton was sometimes dragged by weights she would attach beneath the flow of the dress to prevent from fluttering up. The dark color of the fabric shadows most of the womanly...accents that she wished to cover. Besides, the maroon matched the eyes rather nicely.

Though the necklace was rather inexpensive the emerald encased in the thick gold wires was from the blouse her aunt had thought she destroyed. She kept it as a reminder of family, and kept it close to her heart as a barricade.

Not only that but you must protect her while she is unconscious, Thane. You are the reason why she can somewhat comfortably reside in one location. Chase away trespassers, elude the danger. It's simple! They're just humans after all, and there's only so much a dead body can do.

However once an inhuman force threatens the safety of Cecilia, will you be ready?

The lights were blinding. They came in an unrhythmic barrage of cameras that would at times pause for entire minutes, waiting for me to assume a proper position. Rules Breed Rebels

I felt his nails dig in to my neck as he ran a thumb across my cheek. His fingers picked up bits of my blush and foundation. I knew he was going to berate me on how blotchy my makeup is later. My jaw tightened a notch. One moment Mr. Peterson was feeling me up and the next he was on the floor, gazing up at me with a mixture of shock and anger, his chair knocked over in the corner of the room.

My face was stunned in a look of indifference, and I refused to apologize for my actions as I knew even if I didn't intend to do it, I would have wanted to anyway.

Blood rose to Mr. Peterson's cheeks and for a moment he looked like a tomato being squeezed before he exploded. "Get out! Get out of my studio and never show your face here again you wretched little—"

He snatched a stack of papers lying next to him and tossed them my way, intending to hit me but only managed to scatter them back on the floor. "Out! NOW!"

My feet moved mechanically; I crossed the room quickly as if I could outrun the shame that chased me like a road. I broke out into the streets and was immediately rushed with the air of change, the revelation of a new life.

The soft afternoon rain pelted my dress, the already-thin fabric becoming more transparent. I crossed my arms over my chest and tried to walk with dignity. I felt eyes on me as I turned a corner but the ones that truly burned were from my friends who had come to watch the photo shoot. Shivers trickled down my spine at the realization that I hadn't a clue where I was heading. I was lost in every sense, and the sinking sensation of defeat crushed me.

Silence is True Wisedom's Best Reply

He seemed to give me a look that crossed between amuse.ment and pleasure, as if I passed a test he expected me to fail. "Nothing to say? It seems you know your place well."

Another stroke of my hair. A trace of my cheekbone. His hand traveled to my shoulder, kneading and exploring the length of my arm. The smallest shiver in my spine indicated I knew exactly which direction it was heading but I had already cast around myself a mold of submission. I couldn't escape it.

I blinked the sleep out of my eyes. The room was dark aside from the soft glow of the candles that looked like small eyes watching my every move. The sound of rain pecking at the window soothed me into a momentary daze before being jerked to what was the horrifying reality.

With a fat arm curled around me was Mr.Peterson, lying unconscious on three quarters of the bed. Tears welled up, a scream swelled in my throat, and my heart plummeted to my stomach but all I could manage was a barely audible whimper.

All of a sudden my vision began to lurch and bile took the place of the scream that never escaped. I took no mind of stealth as I scrambled out the sheets, kicking my legs as if paddling a bike.

I crawl-ran across the carpeted floor, disturbing the soft white carpet that looked like a sheet of freshly-fallen snow...pure and white until forced about.

In what seemed to be the dead of night, I ran down the street under the harsh illumination of the streetlamps. They acted as spotlights following me, glaring down with mighty disapproval. My clothes fluttered in my hand and I felt a shirt begin to slip and all of a sudden the world spun and I was on the pavement, blood mixing with tears mixing with the rain.

I wasn't sure how long I was there for. The buzz of a fever trickled in but I was an immovable avatar of my torment, washed out under the glow of the street.

A foot came down before me, a child's foot I believed, and paused. Perhaps someone out on a midnight stroll with their parents and about to report their unusual finding. But the voice that emitted from the body was nothing like a child's. "Get up."

I hadn't the strength. I only managed a croak that somewhat resembled, "No."

."I am here to help you. My name is Cecilia Diane Amielle."

This time I lifted my face ever so slightly, enough to see that above her ankle was just a long stretch of maroon-colored fabric. A low laugh shook me. "Since when does anyone help each other in this world anymore?"

The foot was replaced by a hand, outstretched before my face and waiting to take my hand. "Since people start letting themselves be helped." When I said nothing, she muttered, "You are pitiful."

She woman vanished from the spotlight of the lamp and into the darkness, a darkness that quickly consumed me like the silence I chose to pursue.

She does not heed their word but note that she has not used her abilities since establishing her brothel, therefore there is nothing to worry about.
